PROJECT BRIEFING // CLASSIFIED

In the year 2108, humanity launched its first interstellar reconnaissance mission, the Cygnus Program, led by Dr. Jamie Chen. Utilizing a prototype Alcubierre-metric drive, the crew of the Surveyor One jumped 20 million light-years to a supermassive binary black hole system to study the final stages of a galactic-scale inspiral.

They expected a physics survey. They found a Type III civilization cold war.

THE DISCOVERY

The system is not just a natural phenomenon; it is a celestial engine. Two ancient factions—the Aether (post-physical, advanced observers) and the Phaethon (relativistic, Dyson-scale engineers)—are locked in a million-year-old stalemate over the system’s energy.

The Phaethon have constructed a series of Dyson-scale arcs that harvest the accretion disk’s angular momentum. To maintain the stability of their harvest, they have gimbaled a relativistic jet into a "sterilization corridor"—a focused beam of high-energy particles designed to prune the surrounding sectors of any emerging biological variables that could eventually interfere with the engine.

THE STAKES

Humanity is the variable they didn't model. The Surveyor One team realizes that Earth lies directly in the path of the sterilization corridor. Because of the sheer distance and the speed of light, the "beam" is already on its way.

To the Phaethon, Earth’s destruction isn't an act of war; it is a mathematical correction.

THE NARRATIVE HOOK

CYGNUS is the logistical horror of a countdown. While the crew navigates the crushing time dilation and gravitational shear of the binary system, they must find a way to disrupt a Type III engineering project using Type I technology. It is a story of human ingenuity vs. cosmic indifference. We aren't fighting a villain; we are fighting the math.
