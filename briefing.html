MISSION BRIEFING

In the year 2661, humanity launched the Cygnus Program, an interstellar mission designed to investigate the profound physics of binary black hole systems. Utilizing an Alcubierre-metric drive, the crew of the Surveyor-9 jumped 10 million light-years to study the final stages of a galactic-scale orbital decay.

They expected a physics survey. They found a Type III civilization cold war.

THE DISCOVERY

The system is a celestial engine. This megastructure is the result of an ancient divorce: two factions once shared a common ancestry before the Arc Engineers obsessed over the Dyson-scale harvest of the accretion disk. While the Arc Engineers remained trapped in the time-dilated gravity of the engine—locked in their original mission to sterilize any biological variables that might threaten their existence—the Aether moved on. Having evolved beyond the conflict, the Aether have become indifferent observers of the very engine they once helped conceive.

To protect their system, the Arc Engineers have gimbaled a relativistic jet into a "sterilization corridor"—a focused beam of high-energy particles designed to prune emerging biological variables. Having suffered a devastating attack in their past, the Arc Engineers now proactively disrupt any potential threats that could eventually interfere with their system's stability.

THE STAKES

Humanity is the variable they didn't model. The Surveyor-9 team realizes that Earth lies directly in the path of the sterilization corridor. Because of the sheer distance and the speed of light, the "beam" is already on its way.

To the Arc Engineers, Earth’s destruction isn't an act of war; it is a mathematical correction.

THE MISSION

CYGNUS is the logistical horror of a countdown. While the crew navigates the crushing time dilation and gravitational shear of the binary system, they must find a way to disrupt a Type III engineering project using their own rudimentary capabilities.

It is a story of human ingenuity vs. cosmic indifference. We aren't fighting a villain; we are fighting celestial inevitability.
